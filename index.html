Common Questions

Questions We Get All The Time

Does solar still work on cloudy or rainy days?

Yes. Solar water heaters work using UV rays, not direct sunlight. Even on overcast days Kenya receives enough UV to heat water effectively. Our climate is actually one of the best in the world for solar — you get value year-round, not just in dry season.

Will solar really reduce my electricity bill?

Water heating accounts for up to 40% of a typical household electricity bill. Switch that to solar and you immediately eliminate that portion of your cost — every single month for 15+ years. Most clients feel the difference from the very first bill.

How long will a solar system last?

A well-installed solar water heater lasts 15–20 years with minimal maintenance. Solar panels can last 25+ years. Compare that to an electric geyser that needs replacing every 5–8 years — solar is the clear long-term winner.

Is solar good for rental properties?

Absolutely. Landlords who install solar water heaters attract and retain tenants more easily — tenants love lower bills. It also increases property value. Many landlords recover their investment through slightly higher rent while tenants still save money overall.

What happens during a power blackout?

With a solar backup system, your home keeps running normally during KPLC blackouts. Lights, phone charging, fridge, TV — all on. For water heating, solar works completely independently of the grid so blackouts have zero effect on your hot water supply.

Does solar add value to my property?

Yes — significantly. A home with solar water heating and backup power commands a higher selling price and is far more attractive to buyers. As electricity costs rise in Kenya, energy-independent properties become increasingly valuable investments.
